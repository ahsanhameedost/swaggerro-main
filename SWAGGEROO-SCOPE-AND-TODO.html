2 · The two product verticals

VERTICAL A · B2B  White-label branded merch stores (like SwagUp / SpyGup)

  • Swaggeroo gives business clients their own white-labeled store showing the client's branding only (e.g. "EHR's" logo) — not Swaggeroo's.
  • The client's employees / customers visit the page (linked from the client's own website) and place orders.
  • Swaggeroo curates a design library (~100–5,000 designs); clients pick + customize with their logo / phrases / culture.
  • Revenue share (e.g. $50 shirt → Swaggeroo ~$30, client ~$20) — passive income for the client.
  • Fulfillment = drop-ship for these clients.

VERTICAL B · B2C  Swaggeroo's own e-commerce store

  • Anyone (consumer, business owner, procurement specialist) can order apparel / merch / swag directly.
  • Retail orders (≤ 5 units): fixed published price.
  • Bulk / wholesale: custom quote in 24–48 hours; bulk pricing kept custom & hidden initially.
  • Recurring large accounts (e.g. "Key" ≈ 5,000 Ts/yr): share an annual calendar → pick events + personal items → Swaggeroo builds samples/mockups and fulfills.
  • Must accept both retail and bulk/wholesale orders.

3 · Swag / package builder (specific demo feedback)

Current builder is a front-end placeholder with no backend logic — to be rebuilt custom. Required:

  • Upload a custom logo and preview it on the merch (t-shirt, mug, etc.).
  • Select multiple products at once (t-shirts, goodies, bags) — current build allows only one item at a time.
  • Set quantities (multiple/bulk).
  • Working "Calculate" / package pricing (currently does nothing).
  • Team must first learn the exact intended flow (confirm with Saad/Scott) before building.
